### Literature Review: Ice Viscosity and Rheology

Spent additional time reviewing literature related to ice viscosity and glacier rheology, which are central to the project's inference objectives.

Key concepts reviewed include:

*Ice Viscosity*

Ice viscosity describes the resistance of glacier ice to deformation under applied stress. Unlike many fluids, ice viscosity is not constant and varies based on several factors, including:

- Temperature
- Stress magnitude
- Crystal structure and fabric
- Impurities and water content

As a result, glacier ice behaves as a non-Newtonian fluid.

Glen's Flow Law

Reviewed the role of Glen's Flow Law in glacier modeling, which relates stress and strain rate through a nonlinear power-law relationship.

Important parameters include:

- Effective viscosity
- Strain rate
- Deviatoric stress
- Flow-law exponent

Understanding this relationship is important because viscosity is not directly observable and must instead be inferred from measurements such as surface velocity.
